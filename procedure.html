What does the library do ?

The provided Python library aims to assist individuals immersed in scientific pursuits. This initiative originated when my advisor highlighted a common issue among doctoral studentsâ€”many invest time coding to explore elements in ionized galaxies, only to lose their code in the end. This project endeavors to address this problem, offering a free solution accessible to all. Our primary objective is to support as many users as possible.

In summary, the pipeline involves converting an image to grayscale, applying a Fourier transform, implementing a pass-band filter, and filtering the original image for the specified band. This process enables the focused study of a particular element, such as hydrogen, by adjusting the band frequency.

Subsequently, the masked image undergoes an inverse Fourier transformation, followed by the application of a Sobel filter to delineate the contours of the studied element. Next, skeletonization consolidates these contours into a singular curve.

Ultimately, the contour of the studied element is juxtaposed with the original image, allowing scientists to conduct a more nuanced examination of their galaxy or any other relevant context. This comprehensive approach enhances your ability to study and understand complex images, providing a valuable tool for scientific exploration.

Emission Spectra - How dooes it works ?

Every chemical element possesses a unique emission spectrum, akin to its fingerprint. When this spectrum is analyzed, distinct bands become visible against a background of darkness. These colored lines represent the emitted light, enabling the identification of specific elements present in the ionized galaxy.

With the library, scientists can input the desired frequency range for study. By specifying the maximum and minimum frequencies within the band of interest, they can explore one of the possible bands emitted by the ionized galaxy. This functionality empowers researchers to focus their analysis on specific spectral regions, providing a more targeted and insightful examination of the galactic emission.
